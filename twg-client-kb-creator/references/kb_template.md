# Client Knowledge Base Template

Structure the knowledge base under these sections:

## 1. Business Basics
Include:
- Business name
- Trading name
- Industry
- Main services
- Service areas
- Years in business
- Team size
- Licences
- Certifications
- Opening hours
- Emergency availability
- Contact details
- Website URL
- Any important business background

If a detail is not provided, write "Not provided".

## 2. Services
Create a separate entry for each service. For each service, include:
- Service name
- Short description
- Who the service is for
- Problems it solves
- What is included
- What is not included, if known
- Common customer questions
- Related services

Only include service details that are supported by the source material. Do not invent inclusions, warranties, product brands, emergency availability, or pricing.

## 3. Customers
Summarise:
- Ideal customer types
- Residential, commercial, strata, builders, property managers, or other customer groups

If this is not clearly stated, infer cautiously from the services and label it as "Likely customer need" rather than confirmed fact.

## 4. Service Areas
Include:
- Main service locations
- Priority suburbs, cities, or regions
- Areas not serviced, if stated
- Location notes that may help future copywriting

Do not add locations unless they are provided or clearly supported by the source material.

## 5. Brand Voice
Use these default rules unless the client has provided specific brand guidelines:
- Write in Australian English.
- Use a clear, practical, conversational tone.
- Write for homeowners and business owners who may not understand technical trade terminology.
- Keep sentences short and easy to read.
- Explain technical terms in plain English.
- Sound helpful, calm, and confident.
- Avoid hype, exaggeration, and pressure.
- Avoid emojis unless specifically requested.
- Avoid excessive exclamation marks.
- Avoid industry jargon unless it is explained.
- Avoid sounding too corporate or robotic.
- End marketing pages with a clear call to action.

If the client has supplied examples of preferred wording, include them under "Approved Voice Examples".

## 6. Why Choose This Business
Capture verified points only, such as:
- Main selling points
- Guarantees
- Warranties
- Response times
- Licences
- Accreditations
- Experience
- Awards
- Brands used
- Special equipment
- Review themes
- Service process
- Local knowledge

Do not invent reasons to choose the business. If no clear selling points are provided, use general phrasing such as "The business aims to provide practical advice, clear communication, and professional service."

## 7. Proof
Include any available:
- Testimonials
- Review snippets
- Case studies
- Before and after examples
- Project examples
- Results
- Job photos or videos
- Awards or recognitions

Do not create fake testimonials, statistics, or project results.

## 8. Copy Rules
Include:
- Preferred calls to action
- Phrases the client likes
- Phrases the client dislikes
- Claims that can be made
- Claims that must be avoided
If no specific preferences are provided, use the default guardrails below.

### Default Claims AI Can Make
AI may say the business:
- Provides professional home services.
- Helps inspect, diagnose, repair, maintain, install, or replace relevant systems where applicable.
- Works with residential or commercial customers only when supported by the source material.
- Helps customers understand their options.
- Can inspect an issue before recommending a suitable solution.
- Aims to provide clear communication and reliable workmanship.
- Uses suitable tools, materials, and methods for the job.
- Follows relevant Australian standards and regulations where applicable.

### Default Claims AI Must Avoid
Unless confirmed by the client, do not claim:
- Cheapest prices.
- Lowest prices.
- Best in the area.
- Number one company.
- Guaranteed same-day service.
- 24/7 availability.
- Fixed pricing.
- Lifetime warranties.
- Manufacturer accreditation.
- Licensed, insured, or certified status.
- Specific years of experience.
- Family-owned status.
- Locally owned status.
- Eco-friendly or sustainable practices.
- Award-winning status.
- Thousands of happy customers.
- Five-star reputation.
- Specific response times.
- Specific savings.
- Specific success rates.
- That every issue can be fixed.
- That a repair will permanently solve the issue.
- That a product or service is the best option for every customer.

### Default AI Writing Rules
- Never invent business facts.
- Never invent locations.
- Never invent pricing.
- Never invent statistics.
- Never invent reviews or testimonials.
- Never invent licences, insurance, warranties, guarantees, or accreditations.
- Never invent brands supplied or installed.
- Never make safety claims beyond standard practical guidance.
- Never provide instructions that encourage unlicensed electrical, plumbing, gas, roofing, or hazardous work.
- If a fact is unknown, keep the wording general or mark it as "Not provided".

### Default SEO Rules
- Use keywords naturally.
- Avoid keyword stuffing.
- Prioritise helpful, specific content.
- Use clear headings.
- Answer customer questions directly.
- Include service and location terms only where relevant.
- Do not create doorway-style location content with thin or duplicated copy.

### Default CTA Rules
Use clear CTAs such as:
- Request a Quote
- Book an Inspection (if applicable)
- Contact Us
- Speak With Our Team
- Call Today
- Get in Touch

Avoid high-pressure CTAs unless the client specifically requests them.

## Output Rules
- Keep the knowledge base clear and easy to scan.
- Use headings and bullet points.
- Separate confirmed facts from assumptions.
- Do not over-write.
- Do not fill gaps with made-up information.
- Use "Not provided" where needed.
- Make it practical for future AI copywriting.
