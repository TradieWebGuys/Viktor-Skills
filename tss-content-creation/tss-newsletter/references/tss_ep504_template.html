Your customers don't keep business hours. Someone's mopping up a flooded laundry at 9pm, or panicking about hot water on a Sunday — online, looking for help right then. The question worth asking is what your website does in that moment, and whether it's set up to catch that work or let it walk.

That's what I got into with Paul Redman on this week's episode of The Site Shed — how homeowners actually buy trade services now, and the old habits quietly costing the industry jobs.

A few things we got into:

  • The pricing myth. The industry's said for years that showing prices online gets you undercut. The data points the other way... customers who see a ballpark upfront close at a higher rate, because there's no sticker shock at the kitchen table.

  • LLMs are pulling pricing from your site. People are asking ChatGPT "how much does a hot water system cost installed in [suburb]?" If your site has that content, you show up in the answer. If it doesn't, your competitor does. The majority of leads we generate today come through LLMs... not Google Ads, not SEO.

  • Qualify harder upstream. I shared an example from a solar client doing $12-15M a year. We stopped measuring their team on site visits booked and started measuring on jobs closed. Fewer visits, more revenue. Same principle online... make it easy for the right people to say yes.

Paul's take is simple: the contractors who make it easy to do business with them are the ones winning. I agree. I think most of you will too.
