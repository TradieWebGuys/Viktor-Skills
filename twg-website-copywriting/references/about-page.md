# About Page — Structure & Rules

Read the parent `SKILL.md` and the client's `client-kb.md` first. This file covers what is unique to the About page.

---

**Applies globally (from Core Principles)**

Australian English spelling and grammar throughout
Third person — "[Business Name] provides…" not "we provide…"
Evergreen copy — no owner-name dependency. Copy must remain valid if the business grows or brings on staff
No fluff — every sentence must reassure, inform, or convert. Cut anything that does not
Confirmed facts only — never invent licences, certifications, memberships, awards, guarantees, response times, team size, or values that aren't explicitly supplied in the client KB. If a detail is missing but would strengthen the page, flag it for confirmation rather than filling the gap
Banned words/phrases: "go to", "top quality", "all in one", "trusted source", "top-notch", "clogs", "clog", "meticulous", "seamless", "craft", or any passive voice. Do not begin sentences with "And", "But", or "At"

**About Us — specific rules**

Target 300–500 words total of body copy (excludes headings, CTA button text, and licence line). Scale to what's actually confirmed for the business — don't pad the base five sections to hit a number

300–350 words: solo operator or small team, narrow service range, single service area — the default for most clients
400–500 words: larger team, multiple confirmed licences/memberships, or a broad multi-suburb service area. Reach this by adding one of the optional sections below, not by lengthening the base five sections

Paragraph length is capped, not just page length. Every paragraph runs 2–3 sentences, roughly 40–70 words. Build the page from short, modular blocks broken up by subheadings — never a continuous narrative paragraph. If a paragraph runs past 3 sentences, split it across two subheadings or cut it
State each fact once. A detail — years of experience, brand partnerships, team size, licence type — appears in exactly one section. Repeating it later to fill a section is the single biggest cause of overlength pages
Contradiction check. If both an establishment date and a total years-of-experience figure are used, confirm they don't read as contradictory. A business "established in 2017" claiming "20 years' experience" needs to be clearly framed as combined team experience or the owner's prior industry experience — check the KB for which one applies before writing
Follow-up paragraphs after a bullet list are allowed if they add new information — process, philosophy, a specific commitment — not if they restate the bullets in sentence form. A paragraph that just re-describes what the list already said is the violation, not the paragraph's existence

**Base section structure (300–350 words)**

| Section | Word Count | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Intro | 60–90 words | Business name, location, established year/experience, one earned detail |
| What We Do | 50–80 words | High-level capability summary only — do not re-describe every individual service; link out to service pages instead |
| Why Choose [Business Name] | Bullet list, 4–6 items | Each bullet a short phrase (5–10 words), not a full sentence. Optional short follow-up paragraph — only if it adds information the list didn't already cover |
| Service Area | 40–60 words | Suburbs/region covered, one locally-grounded detail |
| Closing CTA | 30–50 words | One short paragraph, then the CTA line — do not recap the page here |

**Optional sections (400–500 word band)**

Add only when the KB confirms enough detail to support them — do not invent content to reach the higher word count.

| Section | Word Count | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Meet the Team | 40–80 words | Only if team names, roles, or experience are confirmed. Do not invent names, qualifications, or headcounts |
| Memberships, Associations & Licences | 30–60 words | Only if memberships or certifications beyond the standard licence line are confirmed. Use exact names as supplied — do not describe the business as licensed, insured, accredited, or certified unless explicitly confirmed |

**Other requirements**

Licences: Include all client licences — typically near qualifications or trust signals. Format as: [Licence Type] LIC: [Number]. Pull from the client knowledge base. If no licence number is in the KB, flag it as outstanding before writing the page
End with soft CTA to Contact page
AIO: Include specific facts — years in operation, service area, licence type — that AI tools can extract as entity attributes
