# Location Pages — Structure & Rules

Read the parent `SKILL.md` and the client's `client-kb.md` first. This file covers what is unique to location pages.

Location pages require a different differentiation strategy from service pages. The structure stays consistent across a location-page batch — that consistency is correct for SEO entity signals. What changes on each page is the **substance within the structure**, driven by suburb-level research conducted before writing begins.

---

## Mandatory Research Step

Do not begin writing a location page until the following have been confirmed through web research or `client-kb.md`:

1. **Council area** — which local government area the suburb falls under
2. **Property and infrastructure profile** — approximate era of housing stock, dominant construction type (slab-on-ground, stumped, brick veneer, weatherboard), known pipe or wiring materials common in the area
3. **Local conditions relevant to the trade** — soil type (reactive clay, sandy, basalt), topography, proximity to waterways, known local issues (root intrusion, flooding, high water table, pre-1980 wiring, weather exposure)
4. **Adjacent suburbs** — two or three genuinely adjacent suburbs for the "Areas Nearby" close
5. **One locally-specific FAQ angle** — a question more relevant to this suburb than the generic FAQ set (council approval requirements, specific infrastructure age, estate-specific conditions)

If web research returns no useful local detail, use the most specific available inference — housing era from suburb development history, proximity to waterways, council area characteristics. Note where inference has been used rather than confirmed fact.

---

## Differentiation — Content Layer Only

Structural variation across location pages is not the goal and should not be forced. The following content elements must be genuinely different on each page:

- **Opening paragraph** — must include a suburb-specific earned detail drawn from the research step. Not a suburb-name swap — a different fact about a different place. Reader-first pacing from `SKILL.md` still applies.
- **One section per page gets a locally-grounded paragraph** — typically the Capability or relevant sub-service section. This is where the specific infrastructure era, soil condition, or local characteristic lives.
- **The "Areas Nearby" close** — reference actual adjacent suburbs, not a generic "surrounding areas" phrase.
- **One locally-specific FAQ** — drawn from the research step. Different from the standard FAQ set and reflects a genuine local consideration for this suburb.

---

## Page Structure

Same module structure as service pages, with these differences:

- **H1 format:** `[Trade] [Suburb]` or `[Primary Service] [Suburb]`
- **Opening paragraph** references the suburb specifically using a detail from the research step — council area, property type, local infrastructure condition. Reader-first sentence first; technical local detail in sentence two or three.
- **Capability module** lists all services offered in that area, each linked through to its dedicated service page.
- **One section** includes a locally-grounded paragraph drawn from the research step.
- **Close with "Areas Nearby We Also Service"** — reference actual adjacent suburbs with links to those location pages.
- **FAQs** include at least one locally-specific question from the research step.

The locally-specific detail from the research step is the primary AIO differentiator — it is what makes this page citable for this suburb specifically and prevents the page from reading as a suburb-name swap of every other location page on the site.
